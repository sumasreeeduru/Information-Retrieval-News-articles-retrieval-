Local opt outs   Notable presenters      Past presenters     Heads of Radio  Scotland   References   External links   History edit  Main article  BBC Home Service BBC Radio Scotland was founded as a full time radio network on    November          Previously it was possible only to opt out of BBC Radio    and the service was known as Radio   Scotland or  formally on air  as  BBC Scotland Radio      The establishment of a separate network was made possible when Radio   became a fully UK wide network when it moved from medium wave to long wave and new VHF  FM  transmitters were brought into service so that Radio   and Radio Scotland no longer had to share on FM  However it was not until the early     s that Radio   was available on FM across all of Scotland so for its first decade on air  the station only broadcast during the day so that Radio   could be heard on Radio Scotland s transmitters in the evening to compensate for poorer AM reception after dark   Programmes edit  The station broadcasts a wide range of programming  including news  debate  music  drama  comedy and sports   News and current affairs edit  News and current affairs programming has always constituted the dominant part of BBC Radio Scotland s schedules  especially on weekdays  In addition to regular  mostly hourly  news bulletins  the station broadcasts a wide range of longer  more in depth news and current affairs strands  Good Morning Scotland  weekdays             is Scotland s longest running radio programme and one of the most popular in the country  providing regular news  sport  business  travel and weather bulletins along with interviews and in depth reports in a manner similar to BBC Radio   s Today programme  but from a Scottish perspective  It also features the daily religious slot Thought for the Day  again similar to that broadcast on Radio   s Today programme  The other daily news and current affairs strands are the Morning Call phone in with Kaye Adams and Louise White of the major sporting events linked to its coverage area  holding non exclusive rights to the Scottish Professional Football League and producing several editions of its Sportsound programme on a multitude of frequencies on Saturdays during the season  The station also broadcasts live commentaries from both of Scotland s domestic football cup competitions  Scotland football internationals and European games affecting Scottish teams and the rugby union Six Nations championships  Alongside the main live coverage  Radio Scotland also produces popular sporting magazine shows such as football fanzine Off the Ball and the multi sports programme Sports Nation  presented by former Scotland rugby player  John Beattie  Every weekday evening during the football season  Sportsound also broadcasts on MW              looking at different aspects of Scottish football in tandem with live games   Local opt outs edit  BBC Radio Orkney and BBC Radio Shetland both air a half hour daily news programme
