active          Rizwan  Riz  Khan  Urdu              born April       is a British broadcaster of Asian origin  From      until April      he hosted his own eponymous television show on Al Jazeera English  He first rose to prominence while working for the BBC and CNN   Contents    Early life   Broadcasting career   References   External links   Early life edit  Khan was born in Aden  South Yemen  to a Pakistani Punjabi father       and an Indian Gujarati mother     His father was from Amritsar    and migrated to Pakistan during the partition  while his mother s roots go back to Kathiawar  Gujarat     Khan moved with his family to London  at the age of four  He identifies himself as the first mainstream BBC and CNN newscaster of South Asian origin     Khan is a Muslim by religion     He attended both Springwell Junior and Hounslow Manor Comprehensive Schools and joined the Air Training Corps  He graduated with a Bachelor of Science with Honours in Medical Physiology from the University of Wales  and then took a postgraduate course in Radio Journalism at the Highbury College near Portsmouth   Broadcasting career edit  In      he was selected for the BBC News Trainee scheme   a two year BBC training system  usually taking only   people per course  Khan progressed to jobs as a BBC reporter  producer  and writer  working in both television and radio  and would later become one of the founding News Presenters on BBC World Service Television News  He hosted the news bulletin that launched BBC World Service Television News in       In       he moved to CNN International  where he became a senior anchor for the network s global news shows  Events he covered included the      and      coverage of elections in India  the      historic election in Britain  and in April      the unprecedented live coverage from the Muslim pilgrimage  the Hajj  In      he launched his interactive interview show CNN  Q A with Riz Khan  and he has conducted interviews with guests including former UN Secretary General Kofi Annan  former US Presidents Jimmy Carter and Bill Clinton  the Dalai Lama and Nelson Mandela  and genomic scientist J  Craig Venter  Khan also secured the world exclusive with Pakistan s General Pervez Musharraf following his coup in October       Khan also hosted Q A Asia with Riz Khan   These interactive shows put world newsmakers and celebrities up for viewer questions live by phone  e mail  video mail  and fax  along with questions and comments taken from the real time chatroom that opens half an hour before each show  From      until April       Khan had his own eponymous show on Al Jazeera English  in which he interviewed analysts and policy makers and invited viewers to interact with them via phone  email  SMS messages or fax     Khan speaks Urdu and Hindi    and also understands other South Asian languages such as Punjabi and Kutchi  He has studied French  and can understand some other European languages  including Swedish and Norwegian  citation needed  In      he authored his first book  Al Waleed  Businessman Billionaire Prince  published by Harper Collins  In      he authored a preface
