       Sorious Samura  From Wikipedia  the free encyclopedia    Jump to navigation Jump to search  Sorious Samura  born    October       is a Sierra Leonean journalist  He is best known for two CNN documentary films  Cry Freetown        and Exodus from Africa         The self funded Cry Freetown depicts the most brutal period of the civil war in Sierra Leone with RUF rebels capturing the capital city  January           The film won  among other awards  an Emmy Award and a Peabody   Exodus from Africa shows the harrowing effort by the best of young African male blood to break through to Europe via death  and danger ridden paths from Sierra Leone and Nigeria  via Mali  the Sahara desert  Algeria  and Morocco through the Strait of Gibraltar to Spain  In his recent two projects Living with Hunger and Living with Refugees  nominated for an Emmy award   he takes reality television to its extreme  becoming the central character in the films by living the lifestyle of an Ethiopian villager and Sudanese refugee respectively  in doing this  he tries to break the boundary between  us   the people watching on TV  and  them   those before the camera  by becoming one of them  albeit for just a month   Living with Corruption  his latest documentary to be shown on CNN  describes the shocking reality of how corruption is spread across society both in Sierra Leone and Kenya  affecting mostly the poor  In       Samura investigated attitudes to homosexuality in Africa in the Dispatches documentary Africa s Last Taboo  produced for Channel    Samura is also one of the directors of Insight News TV  an independent television production company in the UK focused on international current affairs programming  Samura attended the Methodist Boys High School in the east end of Freetown  As of      update   he works in London  UK  and considers both London and Freetown his hometowns      References edit       CRY FREETOWN   Interview   PBS NewsHour     January       Retrieved    October
